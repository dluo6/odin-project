Shrimp Scampi With Pasta

Description

This dish consists of shrimp scampi, which is shrimp cooked in butter, garlic, and white wine sauce, served over linguine. With a total time of 40 minutes split equally between prepping and cooking, this recipe is quick yet fancy enough for a dinner. This recipe yields 6 servings. See the original website on Allrecipes here.

Ingredients

  • Shrimp: 1 pounc. Large shrimp yield the best results. If bought frozen, thaw the shrimp before using.
  • Pasta: 1 16-ounce package of linguine. Can be substituted for other types of pasta.
  • Butter: 4 tablespoons. Use unsalted butter and add salt to taste when cooking.
  • Extra-virgin olive oil: 4 1/3 tablespoons.
  • Shallots and garlic: 2 shallots finely diced and 2 cloves minced respectively.
  • White whine: 1/4 cup. A dry, crisp wine like pinot grigio or sauvignon blanc are good choices.
  • Lemon juice: 1 lemon juiced.
  • Seasonings: Kosher salt and black pepper amplify the flavors, and red pepper flakes can be added to the shrimp scampi for an extra kick. 1 pinch for each.
  • Parsley: 1/4 cup.

Steps

See the website for tips on success.

  1. Gather ingredients.
  2. Bring a pot of salted water to boil. Cook linguine for 6-8 minutes and drain.
  3. Melt 2 tbsp of butter and 2 tbsp of olive oil over a skillet on medium heat.
  4. Cook shallots, garlic, and red pepper flakes for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Season shrimp with salt and pepper, add to pan and stir occasionally for 2-3 minutes. Remove shrimp from skillet and keep warm.
  6. Pour white wine and lemon juice to skillet and bring to a boil. Scrape brown bits of food with a spoon.
  7. Melt 2 tbsp of butter and stir 2 tbsp of olive into mixture. Bring to a simmer.
  8. Toss shrimp, linguine, and parsely in skillet until coated in sauce. Season with salt, pepper, and remaining olive oil.
  9. Serve and enjoy!
